Common conditions

Common spine conditions we help with

Back pain has many causes. Here are the conditions we most commonly support.

💢

Lower Back Pain

Mechanical low back pain from poor posture, lifting strain, or muscle fatigue. Lumbar bracing offloads the spine and supports recovery.

🔄

Scoliosis

Curvature of the spine — often diagnosed in adolescence. Spinamic bracing has helped many patients reduce curve progression and avoid surgery.

🏥

Post-Surgical Spine Recovery

After discectomy, fusion, or endoscopic spine procedures, controlled support helps the spine heal in the right position.

⚡

Disc Herniation

Pinched nerves and radiating pain from a herniated disc — bracing reduces movement that aggravates symptoms during conservative care.

🧍

Postural Issues

Forward head posture, kyphosis, and rounded shoulders — postural braces serve as gentle reminders during daily activity.

🦴

Osteoporotic Compression

Vertebral compression fractures common in osteoporosis. Specialist bracing reduces pain and supports healing.

        Understanding back pain — and what helps

        Back pain affects most adults at some point. Most cases resolve with conservative care, but knowing when bracing helps — and when to see a specialist — matters.

        Why does my back hurt?

        Most back pain is mechanical — from posture, lifting, or muscle strain. Some pain comes from disc issues, scoliosis, or age-related changes. Identifying the cause matters because treatment varies.

        How does spine bracing help?

        Bracing offloads stressed structures, limits painful motion, and gives the spine the right environment to heal. For scoliosis specifically, bracing during growth has strong evidence for slowing curve progression.

        When should I see a doctor?

        If back pain is severe, lasts more than a few weeks, radiates into your legs, or is accompanied by numbness, weakness, or changes in bladder control — see a spine specialist immediately.

        This page is educational. Spine conditions vary widely. Please consult a qualified spine specialist before starting any bracing programme.
