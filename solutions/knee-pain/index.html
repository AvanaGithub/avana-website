Common conditions

What's behind your knee pain?

Knee pain rarely has one cause. These are the conditions we most commonly help patients manage with the right support.

🦴

Osteoarthritis (OA)

Cartilage wear that causes pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility — especially common after age 50. Offloader bracing can shift load away from the affected compartment.

⚡

ACL / Ligament Injury

Sports and accident-related ligament tears that need stability and protection during rehabilitation and return-to-activity.

🏥

Post-Surgical Recovery

After total knee replacement, ACL reconstruction, or arthroscopy — controlled motion and cold therapy speed safe recovery.

🎯

Patellofemoral Pain

Pain around or behind the kneecap, often during activity. Patellar tracking braces can help relieve pressure.

🩹

Meniscus Injury

Cartilage tears causing swelling, locking, or pain. Functional bracing supports the knee while it heals.

💪

General Instability

If your knee 'gives way,' even on flat ground, you may benefit from a stability brace tailored to your activity level.

        Understanding knee pain — and what helps

        Knee pain is one of the most common reasons Indian adults visit an orthopaedic specialist. The right support, used at the right time, can meaningfully reduce pain and help you stay active — but it isn't a substitute for medical advice. Here's what to know.

        Why does my knee hurt?

        The knee is a complex joint that takes load every time you walk, sit, or climb stairs. Pain can come from worn cartilage (osteoarthritis), inflamed soft tissue (tendinitis or bursitis), ligament injury, or wear from repeated activity. The cause matters — because the right support depends on it.

        How does bracing actually help?

        Modern functional bracing does three things: it offloads the painful part of the joint, controls excess motion that worsens pain or risks re-injury, and provides proprioceptive feedback so your muscles support the joint better. For osteoarthritis specifically, an offloader brace can reduce pain enough to delay or avoid surgery in many patients.

        What about cold therapy and compression?

        For acute injuries and post-surgical recovery, controlling pain and swelling early is critical. Cold therapy with active compression — like the Polar Care Wave system — has been shown to reduce post-operative pain medication needs and speed return-to-function compared with ice packs alone.

        When should I see a doctor?

        If your knee pain lasts more than two weeks, involves significant swelling, your knee gives way under you, or you can't fully bend or straighten it — see an orthopaedic specialist. Bracing supports treatment; it does not replace a diagnosis.

        How do I choose the right brace?

        • Match the brace to your condition — an OA brace is different from a post-surgical brace.
        • Get the right size; an ill-fitting brace can do more harm than good.
        • Speak to your treating doctor or our trained specialists before purchase.
        • Wear it correctly and consistently — bracing only works if it's used.
        This page is educational. It is not a substitute for medical advice. Please consult a qualified orthopaedic specialist before starting any bracing or treatment programme.
