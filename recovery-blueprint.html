The reality of recovery

Recovery is often harder than the surgery.

Most patients are prepared for the operation, but not for the weeks that follow. Five things make recovery harder than it has to be:

01
Swelling

Persistent post-op swelling slows healing and limits movement for weeks.

02
Pain

Sharp post-surgical pain peaks in the first two weeks — exactly when you need to start moving.

03
Sleep disruption

Throbbing and discomfort wake patients every few hours, delaying tissue repair.

04
Reduced mobility

Stiffness sets in quickly. Every day lost to immobility adds weeks to recovery.

05
Delayed rehab

When swelling and pain aren't managed, physiotherapy gets pushed back — and outcomes suffer.

Smarter swelling control

Why traditional ice packs fall short.

If you've been told to "ice 20 minutes every hour," you already know how exhausting that is. Continuous cold therapy was built to solve exactly that problem.

Traditional ice packs The old way
  • Uneven cooling — hot spots and cold spots
  • Need to be replaced every 20 minutes
  • Difficult to use overnight without disrupting sleep
  • Often slip off the joint as you move
  • Mess and water as they melt
Continuous cold therapy The recovery standard
  • Consistent cooling for hours — no spikes, no gaps
  • Designed for use during sleep so swelling stays controlled overnight
  • Anatomical pad stays in place on the joint
  • Used by surgeons and physiotherapists worldwide

Plan your recovery

Knee replacement recovery — week by week.

Every patient is different, but a typical recovery follows a predictable path. Here's what most patients can expect.

Week
1
Pain & swelling control
Focus on rest, ice, elevation, and pain management. Your most important week.
Week
2
Mobility improvement
Gentle range-of-motion exercises begin. Walking with support increases day by day.
Week
4
Strength rebuilding
Structured physiotherapy intensifies. Many patients return to light routines.
Week
8
Daily activity returns
Walking unaided, driving, and most household tasks become comfortable again.
Week
12
Advanced recovery
Full strength building, return to active lifestyle, long-term joint maintenance.
